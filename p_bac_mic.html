Bacterial Microcosm

Generating energy from waste via microbial fuel cells

Shewanella oneidensis MR-1 is a bacterium known for its ability to survive on metal ions in environments with or without oxygen. The bacteria has been industrially used as agents for wastewater treatments.

Inspired by the indigenous approach to aquafarming in East Kolkata Wetland, East India, the project recreates a microcosm of the regenerative ecosystem that leverages the power of microbial fuel cells to generate electricity from waste.

Machanism of a Regenerative Aqua-agriculture

The EKW operates the world's most extensive aquaculture system powered by wastewater. Sewage directed to the wetlands undergoes a process of solar purification, followed by natural oxidation. This treatment renders the water suitable for the growth of algae and plankton, which serve as the primary food source for the fish. However, due to emergent urbanization, the quality of the sewage has changed, which jeopardized the indigenous approach of re-generative aquafarming.
